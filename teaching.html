EIT Teaching and Learning Practice
As a lecturer, I embrace a values-driven teaching approach that fosters respect and collaboration, empowering students to develop essential skills for working effectively with others. By integrating an industry-focused strategy, I equip students with real-world experiences that enhance their confidence in independent learning and prepare them for professional success. I also guest lecture on demonstrations of real industry needs to EIT postgraduate students. I continuously refine my teaching methods through learner feedback and transparent assessments, ensuring my strategies remain effective, inclusive, and student-centered.

As an academic staff member committed to continuous improvement, I actively seek ways to enhance my teaching approach. Based on feedback from my manager, I enhanced student interaction by strategically grouping advanced and struggling students in collaborative projects, fostering peer learning and engagement.

 In my classroom, I also focus on identifying and supporting students' learning needs. Drawing on my knowledge of inclusive higher education, I create a relaxed, safe and supportive environment, working with the Student Support office and learning advisors to assist students with learning difficulties. I ensure that students with disabilities have a learning environment accommodating their needs. For example, I schedule class activities and breaks with their convenience in mind. Last year, in collaboration with Dr. Noor Alani, I extended a test duration from 15 to 20 minutes to support a student who required additional time for reading questions. Additionally, I am deeply committed to student well-being, recognizing that one of my students experiences seizures, I took a first aid course to prepare for emergencies.

Additionally, my active engagement with industry partners helps identify student needs during internships. This allows me to integrate relevant tools into the learning planning (ML&AI course) and ensure that graduates are equipped with industry-ready skills.
 Working with the Hawke’s Bay and Tairāwhiti campuses, I saw an opportunity to take on responsibility for internal assessment moderation and enhancing Maths in IT and Programming Fundamentals courses. I felt well-suited to this role because of my experience teaching and developing courses. I focused on the latest technological advancements and ensuring our delivery met student learning needs and industry expectations. However, at the same time, we must be mindful of students' concerns about AI technology. For instance, transparency is crucial when designing assessments. We discussed these concerns with Dr. N. Alani at an EIT Ngā Hau e Whā Teaching and Learning Conference. Our research highlighted that students feared their assessments might be misclassified as AI-generated work. Addressing these fears through clear communication and fair assessment practices is essential to maintaining trust and academic integrity.

I was also responsible for aligning the Computing School’s interests with the broader educational vision for the Tairāwhiti region. I contributed to developing the Education Needs Report for the Tairāwhiti region, designed and led a workshop on aerospace and our Computing School for schoolchildren, participated in a robotics exhibition, and facilitated potential internship opportunities for our students.
 As part of the wider EIT whānau, I plan and implement flexible learning strategies tailored to diverse student needs. My lesson planning ensures that each session builds upon the previous one, creating a seamless and structured learning journey rather than stand-alone lessons. To bring courses in Programming and Maths in IT closer to industry needs, I developed a Python(Google Collab)-based education that aligns with university and industry standards while considering the hardware limitations at the Tairāwhiti campus. This approach was also based on consultation with other Te Pūkenga institutions. This method has also been successfully implemented in the Hawke’s Bay campus with my colleague John Jamieson on the Programming course. Deeply integrating Python(the most popular programming language today) into the education process provides a clear progression from session to session(rather than stand-alone lessons) and from Level 5 to Level 7, where students begin with fundamental programming(Level 5), reinforce their skills through Maths in IT(Level 6), and ultimately apply their knowledge to complex, industry-focused challenges in Machine Learning and Mechatronics(Level 7).
